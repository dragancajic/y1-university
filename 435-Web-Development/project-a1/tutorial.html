Study Tips for Living in Lockdown

Date written: 2020-10-27

Remote learning can be difficult for a lot of students. It requires you to be more proactive, take initiative in your own learning, and be ready to ask questions if needed. Additionally, whereas with lectures and in-person lessons, it's more difficult to get into the right mindset with learning. For these reasons, it's important to learn how to work effectively while remote learning.

This guide aims to help you achieve success in these times by giving you some ideas on how to improve your workflow, including:

  • Physically and mentally separating your work space and fun space.
  • Preventing any distractions from compromising your workflow.
  • Properly structuring your time.

Following these tips isn't going to fix every issue, but it will certainly push you in the right direction when it comes to studying in these times and in this environment.

Keeping Focus

Properly utilising your time while studying is important. With remote learning comes difficulty with scheduling and organisation, as your work space and home space fuse. What's important, in these times, is to still try to divide these spaces as much as possible.

For example, dividing the space you have into various physical areas, such as having an area for watching TV, an area for cooking and eating, and an area for working, can greatly improve your productivity. It can be difficult to stick to, but having this physical compartmentalisation can get you in the right frame of mind just by sitting down in the right place.

If space is hard to come by, say if an area is shared between a few of these activities, that's alright! There are other methods of compartmentalising, including setting up multiple user accounts on your computer, or scheduling time for work and time for fun. Stopping yourself from deviating while focussing and working is difficult, but it can improve your focus and increase the quality of your study.

Getting Work Done

Work can pile up quickly, even at the best of times. Keeping on track with work can be difficult, and as such, the importance behind keeping on top of your deadlines and assignments is compounded during Lockdown. The single best thing you can do to counter this is to set a goal for yourself to study work some amount each day. It doesn't have to be much, but skipping out entirely can leave you behind, confused, with work piling up. Even a small amount of work each day can help you stay in the loop.

Additionally, distractions can have a hugely detrimental effect to your work during these times. Even at the best of times, having your focus taken away by a notification of an email or a group chat can ruin any flow that you have while working. For this reason, it might be best to set up some kind of do-not-disturb mode on your phone or computer, allowing you to get into a flow while working.

Learn How to Make Mistakes

These times are anything but normal. You've probably heard that saying many times over, but I feel it's worth reiterating. Even with a perfect work ethic and structure, it's likely that you'll not be working at your best, if you can even find the time and energy to work at all. That's okay! This literally is a global crisis, and it's okay to not feel your best. It's totally okay to not do your best at the moment.

Learning to cope with mistakes and failure is a lifelong skill, one that's very difficult to acquire. It may seem unhelpful to say "Don't worry about messing up" when there's potentially a lot riding on you succeeding at the moment, but I feel as though a reminder like this could help: the fact that you're trying is enough. The fact that you're still making progress, however slight, right now, is impressive.
